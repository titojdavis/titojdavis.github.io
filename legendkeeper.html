Homebrew World

As a forever-DM, I get to spend a lot of time thinking about the worlds my players get to play in. When the pandemic hit I had some time to flesh out the world for my college friends, and also because of the pandemic, a lot of new software was coming out that could help me organize it digitally. I prefer to work in notebooks, but I was a couple months away from moving halfway across the world back home. I found Legendkeeper, a software that allows me to create a wiki for the world, and pin locations on a map. It also had tools to hide things from people with certain permissions, so it was perfect for making a D&D world.

I drew up the map, then started in on its occupants. With some help from my players, we filled out pages of the wiki, and as a result, our world feels much more lived in. The continent we play on has multiple cultures within a multitude of species that are all distinct from one another. In addition to the people, there are also articles detailing the history both recent and ancient, Legends the players may come across or hear rumor of, and the factions that belong to the continent.
